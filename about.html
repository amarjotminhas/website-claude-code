Our story

Started with a pickup and a promise

Raymond Kessler founded Ironwood in 1998 with a used pickup, a framing crew of three, and a promise he'd made to his first client: "If it's not right, we come back and make it right — no invoice." That promise is still printed inside the cover of every contract we sign.

For the first decade, Ironwood built a reputation one foundation at a time — custom homes in the West Hills, tenant build-outs downtown, and the concrete work other general contractors quietly hired us to do for them. By 2010 we'd grown into a full-service general contractor with our own framing, finish, and concrete crews.

In 2019, Raymond's daughter Dana Kessler — a civil engineer who spent eight summers on our jobsites before her first office job — took over as principal. Raymond still walks projects most Fridays. He says he's checking the schedule; the crews know he's checking the corners.

Today Ironwood is 32 people strong: estimators, supervisors, carpenters, and concrete finishers, many of whom have been here over fifteen years. We turn down more work than we take, because the only schedule we'll put our name on is one we know we can keep.

What we stand on

Values you can inspect

Anyone can print values on a wall. Ours show up in the contract, the jobsite, and the invoice.

Safety before schedule

Twenty-seven years, zero lost-time incidents on residential jobs. Every crew member is OSHA-10 certified, and any worker — ours or a sub's — can stop work over a safety concern without explaining themselves twice.

Craft over speed

We'd rather tell you a realistic date than hit a fantasy one by cutting corners you'll find in five years. Our supervisors sign off on every trade's work before the next one starts.

Transparent money

Line-item proposals, receipts on every allowance, and change orders priced and signed before the work happens. You will never open an Ironwood invoice and find a surprise.

Accountability in writing

A named supervisor on every job, a fixed completion date in every contract, and a five-year workmanship warranty we've honored every single time it's been called.
